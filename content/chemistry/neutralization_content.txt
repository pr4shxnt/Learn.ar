Neutralization in Everyday Life
This simple reaction called neutralization happens all around you every day.

Soothing Heartburn: When your stomach has too much acid (indigestion), you take an antacid. The base in the medicine neutralizes the acid, turning it into water and salt.

Fixing Soil: If soil becomes too acidic for crops to grow, farmers add lime (a base). This balances the pH so plants can absorb nutrients.

Treating Bug Stings: Bee stings are acidic. Applying baking soda (a base) neutralizes the sting and stops the pain. Conversely, wasp stings are alkaline and are neutralized with vinegar (an acid).

Cleaning Water: Factories often produce acidic waste. They must add bases like calcium carbonate to neutralize the liquid before it can be safely released into rivers.

Dental Care: Bacteria in your mouth create acid that rots teeth. Toothpaste is slightly basic, neutralizing that acid every time you brush.
